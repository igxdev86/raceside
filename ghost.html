A ghost is a horse in the market's top three while the score puts it in the bottom half of the field — or can't rate it at all (no RPR and no Topspeed: unexposed). The score is structurally blind to what moves these markets: stable confidence, breeding, first-time tactics. So the test isn't "are ghosts good bets" — it's calibration: A/E is actual ghost wins ÷ market-expected wins (overround-normalised). A/E ≈ 1.0 means the market prices them correctly and the score is simply missing information; the practical use is the icon split — how much a ghost's presence in a race degrades the icons. History graded at SP with the leak-free engine score (RPR+TS+T14); today's list uses card prices and the full static score, so the rulers are close cousins. Not advice.
